# Temporal Interference Stimulation and Pain Modulation

This project investigates the effects of transcranial temporal interference stimulation (tTIS) on human pain perception using a within-subject experimental design.

## Overview

We examined whether tTIS applied over the left primary motor cortex (M1) can modulate pain perception in a frequency-dependent manner. Participants underwent multiple sessions including baseline pain assessment, stimulation, and post-stimulation testing.

## Participants

A total of 42 healthy participants were recruited, with a final sample size of 32 after exclusions. All participants were screened for neurological, psychiatric, and pain-related conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Experimental Design

- Within-subject crossover design  
- Four stimulation conditions:  
  - Sham  
  - 10 Hz  
  - 20 Hz  
  - 70 Hz  
- Pre- and post-stimulation pain assessments  
- Bilateral forearm stimulation using thermal stimuli  

## Stimulation Protocol

- Target: Left motor cortex (M1)  
- Device: Temporal Interference Brain Stimulator (TIBS-R v3.0)  
- Carrier frequency: 1 kHz  
- Beat frequencies: 10, 20, 70 Hz  
- Duration: 20 minutes  

## Data Collection

Pain was induced using a TSA-II thermal stimulator and rated using a generalized labeled magnitude scale (gLMS).
